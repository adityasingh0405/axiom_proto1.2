# What is Big Data

## Key Concepts

### Definition of Big Data

* Big data refers to the massive volume of structured and unstructured data that organizations and individuals generate and collect.
* [00:34] The example of data generated per minute on the internet illustrates the scale of big data: 2.1 million snaps, 3.8 million search queries, 1 million logins, 4.5 million videos watched, and 188 million emails sent.
* [04:06] Big data is characterized by the 5Vs: volume, velocity, variety, veracity, and value.

### 5Vs of Big Data

* **Volume**: The massive amount of data generated, such as 2,314 exabytes collected annually in the healthcare industry.
* **Velocity**: The speed at which data is generated and processed, such as 600 billion operations per second.
* **Variety**: The different types of data, including structured, semi-structured, and unstructured data.
* **Veracity**: The accuracy and reliability of the data.
* **Value**: The potential benefits and insights that can be gained from analyzing the data.

### Hadoop Distributed File System (HDFS)

* [04:06] HDFS is the storage layer of Hadoop.
* Data is stored in a distributed manner in HDFS.
* HDFS performs parallel processing of data.
* Data is split into smaller chunks and stored on multiple data nodes in HDFS.
